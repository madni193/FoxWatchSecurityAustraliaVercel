Training & Professional Standards

Training is a cornerstone of the FoxWatch approach. All guards hold the appropriate licences for their state, but we go further with scenario-based exercises covering conflict de-escalation, customer service, emergency evacuations and incident documentation. Guards learn how to be visible when a deterrent is needed and discreet when a lower profile offers better protection.

We emphasise clear communication. Guards are trained to provide concise handover notes, structured incident reports and timely updates when something changes on site. They understand how to work alongside your management team and, when required, liaise with police, emergency services and other stakeholders in a professional and calm manner.

Technology & Transparency

Technology is another key pillar. FoxWatch makes use of digital patrol tracking, incident reporting apps and photographic evidence so that clients can see exactly what is happening at their sites. Rather than receiving vague nightly emails, you gain time-stamped records showing checks completed, hazards identified and actions taken.

This transparency helps you demonstrate due diligence to insurers, regulators and senior stakeholders. Over time, data from our patrols and incident logs can highlight patterns such as recurring trespass locations or peak times for unauthorised access. That insight allows us to refine patrol routes and guard placement to achieve better results with the same or even fewer hours.
